Section 1

Why this policy exists

This is not a generic phone policy. It exists because we work with cadaveric human donors under a legal agreement with UCSF, and the people who donated their bodies to your education trust us to protect their privacy and dignity.

These policies are in place to:

  • Maintain the privacy and dignity of human donors. Every cadaver in our lab was once a person who chose to donate their body to your education. They cannot consent to being photographed or recorded.
  • Uphold our legal agreement with UCSF. Solano's access to cadaveric specimens depends on UCSF's body donation program. Violation of these terms can end Solano's access for future students.
  • Protect the learning environment from distraction. Anatomy lab demands focus. Devices fragment attention and reduce learning for everyone in the room.
  • Mirror professional expectations in clinical and anatomy-based settings. In clinical practice, taking photos of patients or specimens is a fireable offense and can be a felony. We're training the habit now.

Section 3

Consequences for violation

Violations of this policy result in escalating disciplinary action. The most serious offenses, involving photography or recording of cadavers, lead to immediate course withdrawal.

Special circumstances: photography or recording of cadavers

Any student who is suspected or confirmed to have taken, obtained, shared, or assisted in the acquisition of photographs or video recordings of cadavers, or who has knowledge of such activity and fails to report it, will be immediately removed from the course with a Withdrawal.

The student will be required to meet with the Department Chair and the Dean. The student will be prohibited from entering the lab for this course, and the violation may impact future enrollment in any Solano anatomy courses.

This policy reflects ethical, legal, and professional standards governing human anatomical donation and is strictly enforced.

Digital device discovery in the lab: second offense

  • Student is immediately dismissed from class and will not be permitted to return for three class days.
  • No assignments, exams, quizzes, or lab work missed during this time may be made up.
  • Student will be required to meet with the Dean of Math and Science before returning to class.
  • Upon return, the student will be required to store their phone in a locked cabinet at the start of each class session for the remainder of the course.
