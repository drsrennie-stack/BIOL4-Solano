BIO 004, Course Reference

Course Materials

What you need (and what you do not need) to succeed in this course. Listed in order of priority.

Textbook (recommended, not required)

No textbook is required for this course. Pick the option that fits your budget and learning style. All three cover the anatomy material we will study.

OpenStax Anatomy and Physiology, 2nd Edition

Cost: Free. Peer-reviewed, available online and as a free PDF at openstax.org/details/books/anatomy-and-physiology-2e. The most accessible option, and sufficient for the course.

Marieb, Brady, and Mallatt, Human Anatomy, 9th Edition

ISBN-13: 9780135240601. Used copies available online. This is the dedicated anatomy textbook (no physiology).

Marieb, Human Anatomy and Physiology, two-semester edition

Any recent edition will cover the anatomy content for this course. If you already own this from another class or a friend, it works.

Mastering A&P access is optional. It is not required for any graded course component. Do not buy it just because it shows up bundled with the textbook.

Lab supplies (needed by week 3)

  • Non-latex nitrile gloves. Share a box with your group to reduce cost.
  • Lab coat (required). Stored in the lab through the end of the term. Cannot be shared with chemistry. You will need your own.
Heads up: The 8-week pace means you need lab supplies on hand by week 3, not week 6. Plan to order or pick up supplies during week 1 or 2.

Required technology

To be successful in this course, you must have access to the following:

  • Tablet, laptop, or desktop computer capable of running multiple browser tabs, streaming videos, and using PDF editing or scanning tools. Recommended minimum: 8 GB RAM, 10 GB available storage.
  • Reliable internet connection for Canvas, lecture videos, and assignment submissions.
  • Printer and scanner, or a scanning app (Adobe Scan, CamScanner) for legible handwritten work.
  • Camera-enabled device for capturing clear images of models, lab work, or assignment pages.
  • PDF conversion tool. All scanned or photographed assignments must be submitted as PDFs through Canvas.

Important: You are responsible for ensuring your devices meet these requirements. Inadequate technology access may result in missed deadlines and academic penalties unless prior arrangements are made.

Optional supplementary materials

These are optional but useful for some students:

  • Krieger, P. A. (2021). A Visual Analogy Guide to Human Anatomy (5th ed.). Morton Publishing. (Workbook with mnemonics)
  • Malakhova, Chinn, Krabbenhoft, & Smith (2022). A Photographic Atlas for Anatomy & Physiology (2nd ed.). Pearson.
  • Complete Anatomy by 3B Medical (App, paid)
