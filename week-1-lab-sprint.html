Section 1

Your week, day by day

Class time is always active learning — never concept lectures. Concept work is night work, done with your workbook and digital atlas the evening before each lab. Pre-study (gold) is a quick morning refresher, in-class (terra) is your Spiral Rounds reference during active work, and night review (navy) is the heavy concept lifting for tomorrow. Sunday is just an organize-for-the-week panel.

Sunday June 14 · before Week 1 begins

Organize

Set up for the week

No pre-study required

Get your tools ready before Monday. Sunday is for organizing, not pre-studying.

  • Skim your Week 1 Research Workbook online so you know what's coming. Your printed copy will be provided in class.
  • Open the digital atlas and click around so you know where to find orientation and planes, the four tissue types, and integumentary structures.
  • Set a regular study time AND build a study environment that works for you (lighting, noise level, devices, snacks). The right environment is half the battle.
  • Class teams will be assigned for TBL, and your team is a strong default study group. You're also encouraged to build your own outside-of-class group with people whose learning style matches yours. Outside-of-class interaction at times that work for you is valuable.
  • Spiral Rounds is meant to run in a group; opting out is highly discouraged. If your group isn't performing, find one that does. You don't have to stay.
  • Plan your Monday morning: room, parking, what to bring.

Section 2

Spiral Rounds (study group method)

Spiral Rounds is your in-class and study-group method, organized by concept category. Each category has 1 or more sets of about 10 to 12 structures. Take each set through the five rounds below, escalating challenge each round.

Open standalone version

Group size: 3 to 5 students per Spiral Rounds set.

Pace: aim for 5 to 8 minutes per round per set. A set of 12 structures runs all 5 rounds in roughly 30 to 40 minutes.

Rule: every student participates. Active retrieval is the point. Mistakes are expected and useful. Don't opt out by watching.

The week's 113 structures are organized into 11 concept categories below. Each category gets one or more sets of 10 to 12. Tick off rounds as your group completes them.
