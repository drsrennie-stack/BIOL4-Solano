Assessment details

Team-Based Learning (TBL)

Each TBL has two graded components:

  • iRAT (individual): a 10 to 15 question quiz on your own. Weighted 3x = 30 points.
  • tRAT (team): the same quiz, taken with your team. Weighted 1x = 10 points.

Total per TBL: 40 points. The iRAT alone accounts for 75% of your TBL grade. Most teams score 90% or higher on the tRAT, so your individual iRAT scores largely determine your final TBL outcome.

After the tRAT, you and your team work through clinical application questions and the Thursday morning case reveal. Application questions are ungraded but you are expected to understand the rationale if called on.

No make-ups. No exceptions. Lowest TBL score is dropped at the end of the term. Use it wisely.
Lab practical exams

Seven lab practical exams: six module exams plus one cumulative lab exam at the end of the term. Module exams happen Monday mornings, covering the prior week's content with 20% spiral review.

Format

  • 50 minutes, 25 stations, 2 questions per station = 50 questions
  • 90 seconds per station
  • You rotate through stations on a timer
  • Identify anatomy from microscope slides, pictures, charts, models, and prosected material
  • Spelling counts. Bring your favorite pen(s). Pencils not permitted.

Conduct

  • Doors lock when the exam begins. Late arrival means you cannot enter.
  • Do not look around the room or at other stations while at your station. Fold your paper to cover your answers.
  • Failure to comply will result in collection of your exam, graded as is.
  • No make-ups.
Cumulative lab exam (week 8, Thursday afternoon)

The cumulative exam counts as your seventh lab exam grade and includes equal representation of material from each module.

Partial grade improvement

If your score on the cumulative exam is higher than your score on a prior module exam, 50% of the improvement is added to your earlier score. The original is not replaced.

Example: 40% on Lab Exam 1 and 80% on the cumulative = 40 percentage point improvement. Half of that (20 pts) added back to Exam 1 raises it to 60%.

End-of-day lab quizzes (community accountability)

At the end of nearly every lab session, I ask one person in one randomly selected group a single question. The entire class earns the same score based on that one student's answer. Each quiz is 3 points.

This format is intentional. Your lab quiz grade depends on the readiness of every student in the room, not just yourself. Show up for each other. Put the work in during lab.

  • Students present earn the class score for that quiz.
  • Students absent earn a zero (no make-ups, per the attendance policy).
  • No dropped lab quiz scores at the end of the term.
Depth of Knowledge (DOK) levels

Most assessment questions are DOK 2 and DOK 3. Questions are designed to prepare you for healthcare applications, not just memorization.

  • DOK 1, basic recall (~10%): naming structures, defining terms.
  • DOK 2, skills and concepts (~40 to 50%): applying knowledge, identifying relationships, interpreting images.
  • DOK 3, strategic thinking (~40 to 50%): solving clinical problems, justifying answers, predicting impact.
  • DOK 4, extended thinking (~0 to 10%): bonus or optional integrative work.
Make-up and late work policy
  • All quizzes, exams, lab reports, and assignments are due by the scheduled date.
  • No make-ups, no late work accepted.
  • All in-class assessments must be completed in person. If absent, you earn a zero.
  • The key: do not miss class.
